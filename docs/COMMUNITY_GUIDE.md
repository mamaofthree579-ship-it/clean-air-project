Community Engagement Guide

The Community Air Project is designed to be used by:
- local residents
- schools
- community workshops
- makerspaces
- small municipalities
- environmental justice groups

## Running a Local Workshop
1. Print or share the Build Guide
2. Have pre-flashed units ready OR flash during the workshop
3. Use the Training Slides (coming soon)
4. Demonstrate:
   - how to interpret PM2.5 levels
   - how filtration changes air quality
   - how to maintain sensors

## Community Best Practices
- Share data openly when possible
- Label deployed units clearly
- Rotate filters in shared units monthly
- Encourage youth participants

## Common Use Scenarios
- Wildfire smoke monitoring
- Indoor classroom air quality
- Community clean-air rooms
- Public health outreach
- Research and calibration studies

## Contributing Back
Communities can contribute by:
- submitting anonymized data
- reporting build improvements
- donating 3D-printable enclosures
- translating documentation
